The Backstory
Genesis & Early Exploits: Started at 14 with a Delphi book and no internet. By college, I was neck-deep in Pascal, VBA, Firebird, and hacking the lab's WinServer 2x network. My first big project? A WoW server (MaNGOS) on C++ where I learned the pain of patching binary files.

The real fun started with Lineage 2. I built a custom Socks4/5 proxy layer to intercept and modify packets on-the-fly. It was pure raw socket magic completely invisible to anti-cheat systems. Later, I built 0-day exploits for Firefox and Adobe Reader by finding crashes via Google and spending nights in WinDbg/OllyDbg.

The "Grey" Era & Lua Mastery: Developing botnets taught me extreme optimization. I integrated a Lua interpreter into a C-core, stripped the parser, and fed it raw bytecode and the result was a 4KB binary. Try doing that with Python or PHP!

I built my own custom cryptor based on a Python-to-Pseudocode translator with binary tree algorithms and heavy obfuscation. I even discovered that bypassing Kaspersky back then was possible just by using CMD/FTP for shellcode instead of standard WinAPI - it simply didn't hook the command line the same way.
